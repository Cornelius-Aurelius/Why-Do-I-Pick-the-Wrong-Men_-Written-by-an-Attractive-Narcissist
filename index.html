You were not tricked.

in the way he avoided emotional questions.
in the way he only texted late.

in the way he touched you like you were everything,

You saw potential.
You saw sweetness behind sarcasm.
Wounds behind his "I'm just not ready" speech.
A future with someone who didn’t even offer you a present.

You just wanted him to change so badly,
you mistook absence for depth,
inconsistency for mystery,
and bare minimum for effort.

A man’s words are only worth what his actions are willing to prove.

You weren’t stupid.

That all the pain before him wasn’t for nothing.

Chosen.

To be chosen.

“Do I even like this man?”

“What’s wrong with me?”
“How can I fix it?”
“Why doesn’t he want me?”

Because deep down…
you didn’t want the truth.

The butterflies.

The obsession.

It was because he activated your nervous system — and your body mistook it for passion.

He wasn’t consistent.
He was unpredictable.

Genuine interest

Clarity about his intentions

Emotional safety

To confuse pain relief with passion.

The “I miss you” text at 11:42 PM

The emoji reply after 3 days of silence

The compliment right after you threaten to leave

Just stop.

Healthy love doesn’t start with a bang.
It starts with a slow build.
A sense of safety.
Clarity.
Stability.

But if you grew up in chaos —
if your first relationships were filled with games, inconsistency, withdrawal and reward —

And silence scares the sh*t out of you.

So you run back to the noise.

And then you call it chemistry.

What you think is chemistry is often:

The rush of being noticed by someone who usually ignores you

Your inner child trying to fix what your dad or first boyfriend broke

The dopamine spike when you finally feel seen by someone who usually makes you feel invisible

It’s not love.
It’s not destiny.
It’s a chemical craving for what you never consistently had growing up.

When did you start associating emotional starvation with romance?
When did inconsistency become a turn-on?
